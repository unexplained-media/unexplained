3 zines about art



I’ve ordered a few zines recently and I really wanted to talk about them because it’s such quality content, they’re definitely worth a look.

The two first ones are both made by @commonthreadspress (which recently changed their name from “madebywomenzines”, which explains why you might still find that label on some of their releases), a British press “making zines about art history, textiles and activism” according to their instagram bio.

The first one is from a serie of a few releases on women in the art field. The one I have is on Tarsila Do Amaral, a Brazilian painter, but there are also zines about Artemisia Gentileshi, LGBTQ+ women artists and many more. Although it’s relatively small, there’s quite a lot of content, some history on the artist, her famous works, important women in her network that have supported and inspired her. There is definitely a lot to go research on. The zines are created by Laura Moseley, and this specific one is illustrated by Fernanda Peralta and designed by India Minter, a bunch of talented women whose work should be supported.

When I’m writing this, the only one still available on the shop is the one on Emma Amos, written by Alayo Akinkugbe, an art historian and owner of the instagram account @ablackhistoryofart, and I’m sure is worth a look as well (which you can find here). I do not know if restocks for other editions will be happening, but if you’re interested in these topics you should definitely keep a close eye on their next releases.

The second one I ordered is called “Radical Possibilities”, a zine on art and queer identity. The content is quite diverse, going from interviews, to portraits, quotes, explanation of different topics, all of this within the queer community. As a person that does not identify with the queer community, I found it extremely interesting and enlightening to be able to consume such content. There is important information about queer history, but also what it means being queer in the present times, and understanding how diverse the queer community is. The beautiful design is made by Alice Daisy Pomfret, also the owner of Akin Magazine, being both a magazine and a shop (find this zine here).

Finally the third zine, unfortunately is in French, but I thought it was still worth mentioning because it is created by (again!) very talented women. In order to fill the existing gap between men and women writing about music, many artists, musicians, singers, label owners, illustrators came together to create this zine. Here again it’s very diverse in the content, some music recommendations, interviews, stories about their experience being part of the music industry. I love how you can really feel that each article is written by a different person, all having their sense of humour and their writing style. The second edition just came out a few days ago which you can find online (here) or in different stores across France.

The women that participated in the first edition are Camille Potte, Leslie Chanel, Cristina Daura, Diana Malatesta, Louise Bouchu, Sarah-Louise Barbett, Maissa Daoudi, Anouck Eychenne, Aubrey Hornor, Marouchka Payen, Hélène Degand, Johann Schreier, Inès di Folco and Félicité Landrivon.
For the second edition you can find Pia-Mélissa Laroche, Jennifer Siegrist, Aude Gravé, Julie Mathys, Gabrielle d’Alessandro, Hermine Demoriane, Ana Servo, Lucile Gautier, Virginia Genta, Natalia Paez Passaquin, Sophie Lecluse, Roxanne Maillet, Cecile Bouffard, Laura Automne, and Félicité Landrivon.

I am so happy to be able to consume such content, that is sharing information that deserves more light, giving a voice to those that have been reduced to silence for too long. Go show some love to all the artists behind those zines, they deserve it! I would love to talk more about zines in the future and diversify my small collection, so if you have any recommendations I would love to hear them!
